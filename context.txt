
ITIL 4.0 - DevSecOps Frameworks
This Blueprint for Value integrates ITIL 4.0 Service Value System (SVS) and ITIL 4.0 Service Value Chain with practical IaaS skills, allowing students to "run IT like a business" without writing code. We focus on the SecOps components of DevSecOps

The ITIL Service Value System (SVS) is a comprehensive framework that shows how organizational components and activities work together as a system to create value. 

Guiding Principles: Recommendations that guide organizational decisions and actions
Governance: Controls for monitoring and directing organizational performance
Service Value Chain: Operating model with six key activities
Practices: Sets of organizational resources aimed at achieving specific objectives
Continual Improvement: Ensures consistent focus on enhancing service delivery

Service Value Chain (SVC) - Six Key Activities:

Plan: Defines policies and organizational direction
Improve: Focuses on continual service enhancement
Engage: Manages stakeholder relationships
Design & Transition: Creates and modifies services
Obtain/Build: Acquires and develops components
Deliver & Support: Provides and maintains services

Phase 1: Service Strategy (The "Why")
Define the business value. Draft due on week 10 (5 minutes per team, presentation in class) 

Deliverable: The Service Design Document  Target Audience: What is the service and who uses this service?  
Deployment decisions : Justify why you are hosting this using the selected approach, your considerations for choosing the infrastructure, including cost and compliance considerations
Quality decisions: What are the spoken and unspoken expectations (dissatisfiers, satisfiers and delighters). Define your CTQs
Service Level Agreements : Define your promises to the end users and stakeholders
Phase 2: Service Design & Deployment (The "Build")
Act as Cloud Architects. Build a resilient infrastructure.

Deliverable: Infrastructure Walkthrough (technical demonstration), Context models and Architecture diagram
Phase 3: Service Transition (The "Change")
Act as Release Managers. Manage risk and change.

The Scenario: Once your service is stable (V1), you must execute a Mandatory Non Functional Change (e.g., "Scale up RAM," "Install a Plugin," or "Patch the OS").  
Deliverable: The Change Package RFC (Request for Change): A document detailing the Business Value, Risk, Impact, and Back-out Plan.  
Baseline: Evidence (walkthrough video) of the setup before the change.
Post change:  Evidence (Walkthrough video) of the setup AFTER the change and clearly illustrate your change working (Not how you implemented the change. For example, if your change involves autoscaling, do not show us the autoscaling configuration, do a load test and show us how your setup actually scales)
(Bonus) The "Rollback Drill": In your change management, show evidence that you successfully rolled back the service after a "Simulated Failure."    
Phase 4: Service Operation (The "Run")
Act as Site Reliability Engineers (SREs) and Helpdesk Agents.
Deliverable: Helpdesk and Live monitoring
(Bonus) The "Incident" and recovery: During your final presentation show evidence or a live demonstration of your incident management or Chaos Engineering
